A content management system is the part of a website most people never see in the pitch and use every single week after launch. Choose it for the demo and your team quietly stops updating the site. Choose it for the editor and the site stays alive.

## Start from the editor, not the feature list

Every CMS has an impressive feature list. Few of them are pleasant to use at 4pm on a Friday when you just want to fix a typo. Sit your actual editors in front of a candidate and watch them publish a real change. Friction you can see beats features you're promised.

## Match structure to your content

Free-form page builders feel powerful and rot quickly. Structured content — defined fields, clear types, predictable layouts — is less flashy and far more durable. Model your content around what it _is_, not where it happens to appear today.

## Keep the front-end free

A headless setup lets the editing experience and the visitor experience evolve independently. Your team gets a clean place to write; we get to build a fast, custom front-end without fighting a theme system. Everyone keeps the part they care about.
