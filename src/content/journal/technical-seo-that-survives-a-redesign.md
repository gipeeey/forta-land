The scariest sentence in a redesign project is "we'll sort out SEO after launch." By then the damage is done: URLs have changed, redirects are missing, and three months of rankings have evaporated. Search equity is easy to keep and expensive to win back.

## Map every URL before you touch a thing

Export your current URLs and their traffic. Every page that earns visits needs a destination on the new site — ideally the same path, otherwise a clean 301 redirect. A redirect map built _before_ launch is worth more than any amount of after-the-fact recovery.

## Keep the machine-readable layer intact

- **Titles and descriptions** carry intent — port them deliberately, don't regenerate them blindly.
- **Structured data** (Organization, Article, Breadcrumb) should ship on day one, not as a follow-up.
- **Canonicals and sitemaps** must reflect the new structure the moment it goes live.

## Treat speed as a ranking input

Core Web Vitals are part of how you're judged. A redesign is the perfect moment to fix them — render-blocking resources, oversized images, layout shift — because you're rebuilding anyway.

## Watch the first two weeks closely

Launch is the start of measurement, not the end of work. Watch coverage in Search Console, confirm redirects resolve in one hop, and fix crawl errors fast. A redesign done right doesn't cost you rankings — it compounds them.
